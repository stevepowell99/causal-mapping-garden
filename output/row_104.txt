**Outcome description**: In November and December 2022, 8 out of 10 Parliament blocs representing 61 out of 128 Lebanese parliamental members signed the amendment of Chapter 7 of the Lebanese penal code. This was during a movement organized by ABAAD in front of the Lebanese parliament, or by visit of the parliament members offices.  
**Significance of the outcome**: With the economc situation deeply affecting Lebanon, and taking into consideration that throughout the decades, key social leaders including religious leaders, political leaders,  and key members had the priviledge of developing and drafting laws that indirectly protected them from any accusation and gave them power in their communities, ABAAD succeded to have parlamentarian members as allies to abolish or amend the Chapter 7 draft law to treat sexual assault as a crime. This change is a milestone towards women’s rights and safety in Lebanon, and a testament to the tangible power civil society to conduct advocacy that impacts Lebanese society. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **:  
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: As part of the 16 Days of Activism-associated campaign, "NO shame NO Blame," ABAAD organized protests in front of the Lebanese parliament on Saturday November 26. During this protest sexual assault survivors, women, and activists gathered to demand amendments to Chapter 7 that would make sexual assault a crime. During this campaign, protesters hung  strong messages on laundry ropes demanding a serious sentence for a serious crime. The main slogan was "Rape is not a matter of honor or dignity. It's a crime." This protest put direct pressure on the representatives of different religious sects to sign and take part in the process of amending Chapter 7 of the Law.
