**Outcome description**: In BF in 2023, the monitors were able to obtain on at least 2 occasions (26 May 2023) the release of at least 25 people who had been kidnapped or arbitrarily arrested by Volunteers for the Defence of the Homeland in Ouahigouya in the North region 
**Significance of the outcome**: In the northern region, the Volunteers for the Defence of the Homeland are often responsible for the illegal arrest of people. Many of the people they have arrested have been victims of enforced disappearance. Others are later found dead.   By obtaining the release of these 25 people, the monitors have made it possible to protect their right to life.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Monitors from the northern region, supported by the MBDHP representation in Ouahigouya, were able to secure the release of 05 Peuhl women and 10 children who had been held captive for 2 days by Volunteers for the Defence of the Homeland (VDP) in the Ouahigouya bus station while fleeing their village under terrorist threat to go to Koudougou.  Between January and March 2023, the MBDHP obtained the release of at least 10 people, including 3 children under the age of 11, after they had been arrested by the VDP.
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Non-state authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: As part of the monitoring activities financed by Amnesty Netherlands, the monitors often accompany victims when they file complaints with the courts. This interaction with the judiciary has enabled them to develop important relations with the public prosecutor at the Ouahigouya Tribunal de Grande Instance. As a result, they have been able to secure the release of people arbitrarily arrested by the Volunteers for the Defence of the Homeland.
