**Outcome status**: Outcome lead
**OH-harvesting period**: harvest 3
**coutnry**: Ethiopia
**Year**: 2024
**Outcome description**: Following a panel discussion, organized by CARD on 15 June 2024 in Addis Ababa on their first-ever consolidated human rights report addressing the violations occurring in Guji, three community members expressed their intention to use the report as an advocacy tool to raise awareness about the situation in their communities.   
**Significance of the outcome**: This outcome lead is significant as the report launch enabled locals to define their own priorities, directly supporting the project's objective of amplifying civilian voices in decision-making and ensuring community-led advocacy. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The report launch was held on June 15, 2024 in Addis Ababa. A total of 53 people have participated, amongst which community representatives, local human rights organizations, the EHRC, the Ministry of Justice, and media outlets. Following the event, three community members have expressed their intention to use the report for awareness raising activities, even though CARD is not yet aware whether they organized awareness creation after (hence, this can be considered an outcome lead). 
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: With the support of AINL, under the SCC programme, CARD convened, facilitated, and ensured inclusive participation at the report launch. In addition, already in February 2023, CARD held a consultation workshop in Addis Ababa with thirty community members including women, community leaders, pastoralists, and academics to identify key human rights issues in Guji. The participants that now expressed their intention to use local advocacy to raise awareness on the situation in Guji also participated in this event. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: No
**Rating contribution**: great
