**Outcome description**: In 2022 in North Beqaa, young participants who previously did not feel safe interacting with other youth from different backgrounds and the trainers, built their confidence to speak more openly and established built strong relationships with other youth. This included a young participant with autism and young Syrian refugees.
**Significance of the outcome**: Fakeqha in the Baalbek District is an under resourced area of Lebanon lacking basic services for youths. This is specially the case for young Syrian refugees and youth with disabilities. For some youth with disabilities, the sessions were the only opportunity outside their home to learn and interact with others. Peace of Art was able to create a safe space for people to build confidence and trust with others.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The youth still communicate and meet informally. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: Peace of Art implemented the Innovation Academy project which allowed 18 young participants from different religious and cultural backgrounds to follow 16 sessions of basic computer skills, 16 English session and 4 storytelling session. These sessions also included many ice breaker and energizers using music, accepting others, nonviolence and gender equality concepts
