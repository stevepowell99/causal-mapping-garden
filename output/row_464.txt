**Outcome description**: In June 2024, the Ninewa provincial Council offically requested from the Directorate of Mass Graves to open Sharafadien mass grave in Sinjar, Sinuni, responding the demands of families of victims.   
**Significance of the outcome**: The Sharfadien mass grave in Sinjar had remained neglected since the area’s liberation in 2014, leaving families of the victims without answers, truth, or closure. This neglect prolonged the suffering, uncertainty, and trauma of families still searching for their missing loved ones. Addressing this site is crucial for truth-seeking and healing, as knowing the fate of missing family members is a fundamental right and a key step in the restoration of dignity, justice, and reconciliation for Yazidi survivors.
**Additional information (use if needed) **:  
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: Our Iraqi partner Al-Mesalla, together with grantee Petricor and a network of advocates, played a direct and leading role in advocating for the rights of families affected by the Sharafaldien mass grave. They organized a campaign that included collecting testimonies from victims’ families, holding a press and media conference, and conducting advocacy visits to relevant government directorates, including the Ninewa Provincial Council. This sustained and strategic effort helped elevate the issue into the public and political agenda, making their contribution high in influencing awareness and potential action on the long-neglected mass grave.
