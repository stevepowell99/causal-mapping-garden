**Outcome description**: In the second part of 2024, Kesh Malek led in NW Syria the building of a coalition composed of women and local youth civil society organisations with the purpose to engage with the islamic committee to address issues around gender.
**Significance of the outcome**: In the course of 2022 and 2023, Kesh Malek reviewed their advocacy strategy to address the gender backlash phenomena occurring in NWS and reflected on how to better work towards empowering women without triggering a backlash. This process led to identifying the need to set up a coalition composed of civil society organizations to engage with islamic authorities and address the issue of gender. Kesh Malek hence led the efforts to set up a stronger force composed of several civil society organizations able to influence and pressure the islamic committee regarding gender issues. Engaging with the Islamic committee to discuss the meaning and the need to address gender dynamics could widen the space for CSOs to continue working on women's rights and women empowerement without backlash. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Regional
**Contribution of the programme**: During 2022 and 2023, PAX organized a series of training workshops for Kesh Malek to review their advocacy strategy. Forming a coalition of civil society organizations was identified as one of the strategies to be implemented to address the issue of gender in NWS. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: DRL provided funding to Kesh Malek to  implement a project to empower women in NWS. As part of this project, Kesh Malek was able to launch the creation of this coalition.
