**Outcome description**: In July 2024, The Martyrs Institution in Mosul, the Compensation Offices in Tal kef and Mosul, and the Department for Persons with Disabilities in the Ninewa Plain announced the decision to relocate or reconstruct and adapt their buildings to improve safety and accessibility for women and persons with disabilities during their visits
**Significance of the outcome**: People increasingly prioritize their safety and dignity when visiting government offices, considering it a major barrier to accessing reparations. For example, an elderly woman ,mentioned that the bad infrastructure services, subjecting her to a deeply humiliating situation that violated her dignity. Similarly, many children and sick individuals face health risks from overcrowding and exposure to the sun while waiting. In Tal kef, the compensation office was located in a single room inside the Iraqi intelligence investigation building, often crowded with detainees—making it extremely difficult and unsafe for women to enter and access services
**Additional information (use if needed) **: For the compensation office of Ninewa, USAID worked on providing the office with all logistic needs
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: network of advocates conducted about 160 interviews inside governmental institutions, then a policy paper were introduced and published in a conference which attended by high level of decision makers, along with about 16 advocacy initiatives focuses on this issues, and precise recommendations were submitted 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: for the Tal kef office No, but for Mosul other NGOs highlighted same issue
