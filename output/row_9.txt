**coutnry**: DR Congo
**Year**: 2021
**Outcome description**: In 2021, two communities of Isangi that are involved in an armed conflict over land, reached an agreement to resolve their differences. In 2022, the communities took steps to address last bottlenecks of the agreement's implementation. 
**Significance of the outcome**: The Ifiti community of Isangi was affected by floods and fled into the forest owned by the Bolambi community. The subsequent violent conflict was pacified and the communities started to work on a structural and sustainable solution of the underlying problem; being the increase of floods because of climate change and the settlements on the banks of the Congo river.  
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The program was evaluated in 2022, and the recommendations are currently being implemented. This includes interventions to lift the last bottlenecks of the implementation of the agreement. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Non-state authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: PAX and its partner CDJP-Kisangani worked persistently on this issue for several years, with several workshops and dialogue meetings with local communities and stakeholders. In 2022, this included commissioning an independent on-the-ground evaluation by experts from Tropenbos-RDC, based in Kisangani and implementing their recommendations; the CDJP-Kisangani team went several times back to Isangani (including once with a PAX staff) and also worked on this issue with communities' representatives in Kisangani.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, the local Catholic Bishop and other church leaders were also involved, but PAX and CDJP-Kis' actions were incrimental in reaching sustainable results.
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: Internal PAX and CDJP-Kis reports, and 2022 evaluation by Tropenbos consultants.
