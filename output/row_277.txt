**Outcome description**: In October 2023, the Head of Gender, Family and Child Service of Masisi territory formally expressed a positive reaction to ASSODIPs report on forced and early marriage.
**Significance of the outcome**: This effect reflects the interest shown by the head of the territorial Gender, Family and Children department in reports addressing the problem of forced and early marriages in Masisi territory.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: This approach raises the authorities' awareness of human rights issues that specifically affect women. As a result of the war, women displaced to different parts of the country and those living in rebel-controlled areas have become more vulnerable to sexual violence. However, where there is a bit of a lull, they are involved in commercial and agricultural activities, and are increasingly present in government services.
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: The program supports MDR activities and the use of this information in lobby and advocacy activities. Sending reports on HR violations (and specifically in this case on forced and early mariages) are amongst these activities. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: ASSODIP collaborates in the SCC program with UFAREP for the MDR activities which gives input to other activities. 
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: Draft report and letter available at ASSODIP
