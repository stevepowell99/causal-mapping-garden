**Outcome description**: On 30 May 2024, the President and CEO of the Civil Coalition for Peace (CAP) in Sana'a, Yemen, were arrested  by the security  authorities, against the backdrop of the prominent role played by the Coalition in carrying out advocacy activities that led to a direct suspension of work. 
**Significance of the outcome**: This incident negatively and significantly affected the quality of outputs and planned results, disrupting the institutional work course, in addition to undermining efforts to promote community peace through these projects, reflecting the fragility of the security environment for civil organizations operating in conflict areas. The level of significance of this result is high, as this result led to a complete cessation of CAP activities, as well as the cessation of other local organizations to carry out similar activities.
