**Outcome description**: In May 2024, at the UNSC Protection of Civilians (PoC) week in New York, UN Secretary General's PoC report included strong references to environmental damage from conflicts as a source of civilian harm, echoed by several States in their statements during the PoC debate and related side events.
**Significance of the outcome**: This development helps to anchor the incorporation of the EPS (Environment, Peace and Security) agenda into the PoC agenda and debates, placing this issue increasingly within the purview of UNSC Council - despite the opposition from certain Council Members and contributing to the consolidation of international attention and support for addressing it. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: International community
**Scale**: International 
**Contribution of the programme**: PAX's contribution is direct and indirect. On the one hand, PAX provided input to the Secretary -General's team working on the report, ensuring the key environmental issues are reflected. On the other hand, PAX's long-standing work on the issue has been helping to raise awareness about it for years among Member States, which indirectly paves the ground for more political recognition. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, there are numerous other actors involved in pushing for the recognition of the EPS-related issues on the UN level, including champions among Member States, IOs, and other civil society actors.
**Rating contribution**: little
**The change was unintended**: NO
