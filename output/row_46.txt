**coutnry**: Ethiopia
**Year**: 2022
**Outcome description**: In November 2022, following a production and launching of a documentary movie on the prevalence of Gender Based Violence and challenges faced by Women Human Rights Defenders in Ethiopia, young Women Human Rights Defenders were inspired and committed to the movement. Women Human Rights Defenders also made commitments to work on Gender Based Violence issues in Ethiopia.  The event convened 59 participants including gov't officials, CSOs, WHRDs and other actors
**Significance of the outcome**: Before the production of the documentary there was no organized documentation on the stories and experiences of Women Human Rights Defenders as well as the prevalence of Gender Based Violence in Ethiopia. The documentary highlighted the magnitude of Gender Based Violence and the need for urgent intervention.  
**Additional information (use if needed) **: As part of the 16 days of activism against Gender Based Violence in 2022, a documentary  movie was produced and launched by Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network and Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders Center showcasing the struggle and challenges of Women Human Rights Defenders and women's rights movement and prevalence of Gender Based Violence in Ethiopia. Following the launch of the documentary, there was  solidarity and networking between young and prominent Women Human Rights Defemders. 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: In November 2022, OHCHR in partnership with its partners, Ethiopian Human Rights Defenders Center( Secretariat of Ethiopian Women Human Rights Defenders Network) Ethiopian Human Right Commission and DefendDefenders(East and Horn of Africa Human Rights Defenders Project) commissioned the production of and  launching the documentary film on women rights movement in Ethiopia as part of the celebration of International Women's Human Rights Defenders Day. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Contribution and collaboration with United Nations Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights -Ethiopia and 7 women-led Civil Society Organisations. United Nations Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights -Ethiopia provided additional finances, Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network and 8 Civil Socity Organisagtions provided information, footages and participated in interviews. 
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: The movie was broadcasted live on Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders Center facebook page
