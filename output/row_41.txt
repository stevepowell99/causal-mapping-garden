**FragmentID**: SCC-INT2305/ESF016
**Outcome status**: Outcome
**OH-harvesting period**: harvest 1
**coutnry**: International / External Stress Factors
**Year**: 2021
**Outcome description**: Between 2020 and 2022 the largest insurers in the Netherlands had improved their policies on 195 points, including 10 on weapons policies and 22 on human rights. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Policy improvement is the first step towards improvement of practice around preventing, mitigating and remediating human rights violations
**Additional information (use if needed) **: (-) 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Companies
**Scale**: Organisation
**Contribution of the programme**: The Fair Insurance Guide of which PAX is part (with a focus on arms and human rights) assesses and scores the investment policies of the largest 10 Dutch pension funds since 2013. In doing so it helps create a race to the top, which incentives insurers to improve their policies
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: PAX also participates in the tripartite agreements with the government and insurance sector, focused on implementation of international human rights standards (UNGPs and OECD Guidelines). This also helped improve policy. And: other CSO's also push for policy improvements, though often on other issues (e.g. climate)
**Rating contribution**: moderate
**The change was unintended**: NO
