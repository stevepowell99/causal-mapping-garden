**Outcome description**: From June 17 to 21, 2024, CARD ran the #VoiceForGuji online campaign on all social media platforms in Amharic, English, and Afaan Oromo to raise awareness on the human rights violations in Guiji, reaching 38,237 people, which increased the visibility of the Guji conflict and encouraged public discussion .
**Significance of the outcome**: Previously, the human rights crisis in Guji received little public attention. The campaign increased its visibility and encouraged public discussion, aligning with the goal of mobilizing support for accountability.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The increased visibility of the online campaign is supported by several indicators. We tracked engagement metrics such as the number of views, shares, likes, and comments on posts related to the campaign across platforms like Facebook, Twitter/X, TikTok and Instagram. On social media, we observed user engagement, including comments, reposts with commentary, and discussion threads centered on the campaign’s core messages. In addition, we also received media inquiries and feedback through our official communication outlets, such as direct messages and emails, both during and after the campaign.
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: National
