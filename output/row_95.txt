**coutnry**: DR Congo
**Year**: 2022
**Outcome description**: In September 2022, the Governor of the Province of North Kivu officially instructed the administrator of the territory of Masisi to remove all road barriers from the Chiefdom of the Bashali.
**Significance of the outcome**: Free movement and a decrease in impoverishment in the community. Decrease in cases of arrests and the stresses that result from these roadblocks. The Provincial Governor reacted against these practices by giving written instructions to the Territory Administrator of Masisi to remove the roadblocks in the Bashali chiefdom.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Roadblocks are numerous and mainly owned by soldiers and armed groups. They constitute places of human rights violation and abuses of users of the roads.
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: Assodip organized an advocacy meeting in Kitshanga on June 04, 2022. ASSODIP communicated in the following weeks the resolutions of this meeting to the local authority and copied to the Governor of the Province the resolutions of this meeting and shared informally this relationship with the Governor's Human Rights Advisor.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: The local authority subsequently convened a community-wide safety meeting around the issue. A list of roadblocks was established in this meeting and sent to the Governor. The latter wrote to the administrator of the Territory of Masisi to remove the roadblocks that there were in the Bashali chiefdom.
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: On request: copy of the governor's letter, liste of barriers that was established, ASSODIP's reports on road barriers, official receipts of reception of letters by the governor.
