**Outcome description**: In April 2025, eight (8) offices in Juba have been establised initiated by female lawyers under the Women Advocates Association to render legal aid to female victims  
**Significance of the outcome**: Since the subgranting by PAX to Women Advocates Association of South Sudan (WAASS), this is the first time that the capacitated females advocates have established legal offices to provide probono services.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: The work of the Women Advocates Association with technical support of PAX in 2024 and 2025 contributed to this result because, the first time women laywers with different legal backgrounds (East African trained laywers and Khartoum trained lawyers) have been having opportunities to connect and discuss among themselfves the different challenges, perspectives and practices to better jointly address the weak South Sudanese context of legal juridisdisction and performance including brainstorming on some of the contextualised actions needed. Eight new offices have been created
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Next to PoV-SCC support to women advocates programming, PAX also seeked additional German funded support to enlarge some of the training activities for women advocates, parliamentarians and advocacy groups contributing to this outcome as well.
**Rating contribution**: moderate
