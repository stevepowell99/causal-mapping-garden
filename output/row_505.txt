**Outcome description**: On the 12th September 2024, during an interview on Eye Radio, the director of public relation of the National Security Service (NSS) of South Sudan publicly announced an establishment of a non-summary tribunal to discipline members of the NSS who are suspected of committing violations on human rights. 
**Significance of the outcome**: In the past, Human Rights Defenders faced frequent attacks, often carried out by National security service officers acting independently and without fear of consequences. However, since the establishment of the tribunal, HRDs, journalists, and activists now have a reliable mechanism to hold these officers accountable.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The tribunal has been operational but behind close doors.The Director in his July 2025 interview confirmed previous prosecutions of a number of NSS officers where a number of them had already been dismissed.
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: The South Sudan Human Rights Defenders Network (SSHRDN) organized and conducted multi-stakeholder dialogues during the commemoration of the International Day of Democracy (IDD) on the 12th, September 2024. In the dialogue,  cases documented by 15 monitors on civic space violations space were presented by the SSHRDN secretariat, implicating NSS officers. The NSS director of public relation was a guest speaker and he made the the announcement during the event. He later made a public announcement during his on-air interview with Eye Radio in July 2025.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: October 2023 that highlighted the NSS gross levels of violations on the South Sudanese human rights defenders. other interventions include;Diplomatic and international community’s joint statements regarding the deplorable state of civic space since 2023 calling for the amendment of the National Security Service Act. Additionally, the IDD commemoration day was funded by the SSHRDN main project funded by the Juba-Embassy.
