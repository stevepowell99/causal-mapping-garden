**Outcome description**: Human rights defenders (HRDs) who are members of the National Coalitions of Burkina Faso, Mali, and Niger have enhanced the capacities of human rights defenders and local partners in physical and digital security in Burkina Faso, Mali, and Niger since 2023.
**Significance of the outcome**: Before 2023, HRDs in Burkina Faso, Mali, and Niger belonging to the aforementioned coalitions, did not have the capacity to identify risks, analyze HRDs' security needs, or provide training in digital and physical security.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Following the physical and digital security training held in November 2022 in Lomé, Togo, human rights defenders from Burkina Faso, Mali, and Niger belonging to the aforementioned coalitions, were not only able to identify security risks and training needs in physical and digital security of other HRDs and journalists, but also to train them on this matter. In Burkina Faso, HRDs who are members of the Burkinabe Coalition of Human Rights Defenders (CBDDH) trained journalists under the supervision of FREEDOM HOUSE in May 2024. In Mali, HRDs who are members of the Malian Coalition of Human Rights Defenders (COMADDH) trained AMNESTY partners (TRIJEUD and ADD) in physical and digital security in April 2023. In 2025, COMADDH trained HRDs in the capital city of Bamako in May 2025 in physical and digital security. In Niger, the network of HRDs (NDDH) trained ROTAB monitors and staff, partner of AMNESTY and PAX, in July 2024 in Niamey, Niger. RNDDH also trained HIVOS staff in June 2024.
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: The program trained members of national coalitions of human rights defenders (CBDDH, COMADDH, RNDDH) using the train-the-trainer model.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: No
**Rating contribution**: great
