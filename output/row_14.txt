**Outcome description**: In December 2021, the army in Goli boma, Tore Payam of Yei River County resolved  in Civil-Military peace dialogue to allow unhindered movement of civilians and humanitarian agencies along the Yei -Tore road. This has resulted to easy facilitation of the preliminary survey in Goli and the renovation of  facilities of Emmanuel Christina College (ECC) 20 miles Yei- Maridi road),
**Significance of the outcome**: In the past the road was not accessible for organizations. The military, chiefs and community have agreed not to harm any returnee. This is providing confident to the civilians and has caused more ease among the population. Hindrance was coming from both sides; robing by rebel side, ambushes. Now agreements with the military government side enabled more freely movement. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: Reconcile conducted consultative meetings with community and military. A training was conducted in Goli – trauma-healing & awareness, reconciliation. Finally, a community dialogue was conducted in which it was clarified that some participants were blamed while they were innocent. Reconcile is seen as a gateway to many other organizations in Yei. Reconcile is trusted. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: the army in Goli boma, Tore Payam of Yei River County
