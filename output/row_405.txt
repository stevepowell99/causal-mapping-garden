**Outcome description**: In November 2023, HURIA issued its first written submission to the United Nations Working Group on Enforced or Involuntary disappearances on the state of Enforced Disappearances in Kenya. In the submission, HURIA detailed the overall patterns on EJE and ED cases documented in Kwale and its impact on family members.
**Significance of the outcome**: The change is significant as it shows HURIA'S  improved capacity to engage in international  advocacy efforts to push for redress for families impacted by EJK and ED in Kwale county, in line with one of the objectives of the SCC project
**Additional information (use if needed) **: In July and November, 2023 HURIA participated in 2 Advocacy trainings supported through the project. In the trainings, HURIA gained knowledge of the existing international avenues available to seek redress for EJE and ED concerns. In November, Amnesty provided information on the WGEID interest to receive submissions on ED cases in Kenya. As such, Amnesty convened the November advocacy training where we drafted the submission to the WGEID.
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: SCC project provided financial and technical resources for the Advocacy trainings, and the writeshop to write the submission. Liana Rodrigues provided the contacts to the WGEID for submission of the petition
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Amnesty's EARO has linked up Liana of HURICAP with members of the working group on enforced dissapearance
