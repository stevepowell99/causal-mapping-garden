**Outcome description**: In October 2022, the HRC renewed the mandate of the SR on Burundi following advocacy engagements with various diplomats in Geneva on the margin of the 51st HRC session.
**Significance of the outcome**: Since the beginning, Burundi government opposed the SR mandate and refused the visit of the SR on its soil. There were efforts by the Burundian government to ensure that the HRC does not renew the mandate of the SR. The mandate of the SR is important because it is the only independent mechanism available to monitor the situation of human rights in Burundi.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: While advocating for the mandate renewal, HRDs presented various evidences showing that the human rights situation in Burundi is still volatile and the fact that victims of CRSV do not feel safe to return to Burundi. The pepetrators are the ones in power or protected by those in power. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: International community
**Scale**: International
**Contribution of the programme**: Through the programme, funds were provided to support 2 W/HRDs to attend the 51st HRC session and lobby diplomats for the renewal of the mandate.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Other Burundian HRDs, Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, CIVICUS etc..jointly supported the advocacy process for the mandate renewal. 
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: Statements, panel at Palais des Nations, HRC resolution renewing the mandate.
