**Outcome description**: In 2024, Remembering The Ones We Lost in partnership with Every Casualty Counts, issued a joint statement during the Interactive Dialogue in Office of the High Commissioner of Human Rights (OHCHR) in South Sudan Human Rights Council in Geneva, requesting the Government of South Sudan to establish a comprehensive and adequately resourced mechanism to document the persons killed during armed conflict. 
**Significance of the outcome**: This outcome is very important to the work of the organisation because it shows recognition and acceptance to the work of Remembering The Ones We Lost. It also brought the agenda of South Sudan, which was considered a forgotten crisis, back at the international level.  
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: International 
**Contribution of the programme**: Remembering The Ones We Lost carried documentation of conflict-related killings in South Sudan from 2014 with total of over 25,500 victims killed and some disappeared (presumed dead). These names were verified and published on the organization website. Many governments picked interested in the published data including the Government of the United States of America. Link-minded NGO and Network picked interested on the work of the organization and request for collaboration and cooperation. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: The name of the organisation appeared in the USA Human Rights Report on South Sudan 2022, and this has contributed positively in raising the profile of the organisation, and many other organisations started cooperating with the organisation such as Every Causality Counts through which the voices of people were heard at Geneva. 
**Rating contribution**: moderate
