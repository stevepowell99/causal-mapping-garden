**Outcome description**: Since March 2023, there has been an improvement in the number and quality of proposals, reports written and online advocacy by the EHRDC staff in Ethiopia. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Since inception, EHRDC has had challenges relating to staff capacity in proposal development, quality reporting and management of data resulting in the loss of data and difficulty in retrieving data after a while. This made it difficult to present quality reports, quality proposals and ability to do data visualisation for media products and reports. The Center also experienced loss of critical emails, data, contacts , and communication channels. The training in data management was important in building capacity of the Center staff to reduce their  vulnerability and mitigate the risks of lossing data and better data management infrastructure. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **:  Folearly/smalling the L&A capacity assessment conducted in 2022, and a folearly/small-up training session on  data management held in March 20-23, 2023,  there has been an improvement in the visualisation of data for reporting and proposal writing by EHRDC staff  in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. The EHRDC staff have significantly improved data handling and communication processes. With enhanced skills in data management, EHRDC efficiently organizes and utilizes critical information pertaining to human rights defenders and the organization. The documented data enables EHRDC to engage in online advocacy with stakeholders, including government entities and law enforcement agencies. As a result, the evidence compiled by EHRDC plays a pivotal role in supporting prosecutions and ensuring accountability for perpetrators of human rights abuses. Improving data management and internet resilience are essential for EHRDC to fulfill its program objectives effectively and ensure the security and integrity of its information assets.
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: The SCC program (DD and AINL) conducted an L&A capacity assessment in 2022 upon which a Training on Data management was organised and facilitated in March, 2023. DefendDefenders also provided support in Security Audit to EHRDC  that helped in identifying the gaps and challenges of the organization's data management.  In addition, DD also provided digital security equipments and softwares such as VPN
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: In addition, Civil Rights Defenders also provided support with licenced anti-virus.
