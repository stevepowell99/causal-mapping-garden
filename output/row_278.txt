**Outcome description**: On 21 December 2023, the Governorate of North Kivu Province issued a written order to the administrators of the Nyiragongo and Masisi territories to close underground detention facilities. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Official reactions to reports of human rights violations were rare. The most frequent reactions were denials, often made in the media. Such underground detention facilities operated outside of the legal framework and were closely associated with human rights abuses against detainees. It was therefore important to close these illegal detention facilities.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The Governorate's order was issued in letter no. 01/1932/CAB/GP-NK/2023, dated 21 December 2023, in response to a letter from ASSODIP addressed to the Commander of Operations Sokola 2, with a copy to the Military Governor of North Kivu Province, dated 14 December 2023. This came after ASSODIP and community leaders in Kibati raised the issue at an advocacy panel held on the 13th of the same month in Monigi.
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: These advocacy activities were carried out entirely by ASSODIP.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, during the same period, the core of civil society, the driving force behind Kibati, followed ASSODIP's lead in denouncing the underground dungeons in Kiboro.
