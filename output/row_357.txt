**Outcome description**: Since the HRD Award ceremony and recognition for Wondimu Ibsa in January 2023, Ibsa has expanded the provision of pro bono services to prisoners in Addis Ababa and Oromia region.
**Significance of the outcome**: HRDs continue to be at risk and receive threats due to their human rights work in Ethiopia. For Wondimu, the situation was not different as he was severally arrested and charged for his work. After the award and recognition of Mr. Wondimu as a HRD, Wondimu has remained steadfast in promoting and protecting human rights. The visibility arising from the award has improved his allies and support of CSOs and media. Moreover the award empowered him to expand his work beyond legal defense, fostering collaboration with various stakeholders.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Before the award he provided pro bono legal representation to more than 565 litigants before courts of law in Addis Ababa. These litigants included, HRDs and Journalists.  Despite facing arrest and a 4-month sentence in prison in December 2022,  the civil society organizations and fellow human rights defenders were able to greatly condem the arrest and secured his release within 19 days. Wondimu Ibsa was recognised as an outstanding HRD of the year in 2023. Since the awarding ceremony, he has expanded the scope of the provision of probono legal representation to prisoners in Addis Ababa, Assosa and Oromia regions. He facilitated book donations to the prison administration. Furthermore, the award instilled courage for continued activism in the future. This award has increased his visibility on media platforms to dialogue on transitional justice, civil and political rights.
