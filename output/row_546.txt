**coutnry**: South Sudan
**Year**: 2025
**Outcome description**: In March 2025, the St Thomas Primary and Secondary schools in Kuron Peace Village admitted 110 children from the Jie and Murle communities. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Prior to this, There were no many children from these communities studying in the schools due to the violence existing among the Toposa, Jie and Murle. The children from the Jie and the Murle were not allowed by the Toposa community to study in these schools because they were regarded as children of the enemies
**Additional information (use if needed) **: More than 10 decades, the Toposa, Jie and Murle have been fighting over cattle, child abductions and revenge attacks. The Toposa threatened to kill or abduct any child from the Jie and Murle communities because they were regarded as enemies
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: There were 3 dialogue meetings conducted in July, September and November 2024 to solve outstanding issues existing among these warring communities. The dialogues encouraged common settlement and having children from these communities studying together
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: no
**Rating contribution**: great
**The change was unintended**: NO
