**Outcome description**: In May 2025, Women  Association in South Sudan, convened 39 local chiefs representing 17 ethnic groups in Juba who  agreed for the first time  to  administer balance between the customary laws and the statutory laws and constitution in administration of law in their customary courts in Juba in a changing South Sudanese modern society. This resulted in a call for a National conference on customary laws to be organised by the Council of Traditional Leaders (COTAL) together with the council of chiefs (and other stakeholders) pertaining to early and enforced marriage to be conducted before the end of year. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Bringing toghter a group of traditional chiefs representing 17 ethnic groups customary courts in Juba to discuss join strategy to bend customary courts rules with statutary laws was very important to address growing need for women and girls rights before the customary courts jurisdictions
