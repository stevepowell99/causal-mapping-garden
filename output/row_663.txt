**Outcome description**: In February 2024, a male HRD in Ethiopia launched an initiative called Wendata to challenge harmful gender norms through social media, a space dominated by women and girl-led efforts. This followed a training in militarised masculinity.  
**Significance of the outcome**: Traditionally, advocacy for women and girls' rights in Ethiopia are women-led effort. However, following a training in militarised masculinity, a male HRD was able to establish a space that has enable men to engage in similar advocacy for women and girls rights, focusing on eliminating gender norms on social media.  It institutionalizes a model where men lead efforts to advocate for women’s rights is appreciated as an approach to tackle the negative gender norms. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The Male HRD developed a deep understanding of the linkages between gender, masculinity, patriarchy, and feminist peace after completing training in a pilot project on militarized masculinities. 
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: Training in militarised masculinity is carried out by ABAAD from January 31-February 3, 2024, in Addis Ababa. This introduced HRDs to new knowledge on militarized masculinity and empowered them to actively engage in addressing gender norms.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: The Civil Society Resource Center (CSRC) further supported this initiative to register through its CSO Incubation Lab, while Paradigm Initiative, via DRIF2025, provided a platform for practical experience-sharing with experienced civil society organizations and advocates in digital advocacy.  
**Rating contribution**: moderate
**The change was unintended**: NO
