**Outcome description**: Due to HURIAs sustained efforts to monitor, document and expose EJE and ED cases in Kwale from 2018-2023 as well as its approach to evidence-based advocacy; in September 2023 The Kwale county Anti-Terrorism Police Unit (ATPU) officer attended for the first time a meeting in which EJE and ED cases were discussed. 
**Significance of the outcome**: The Anti-terrorism police unit are regarded as alleged perpetrators to EJE and ED cases, due to their mandate on counter terrorism operations. One of the objectives of the project was to stimulate state actors to respond and address the plight of victims and families of EJE and EDs. As such, their participation in meetings are important because they get to hear from affected families and Civil society on the impact of human rights violations due to their operations. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The Kwale county ATPU officer in charge- Mr. Julius Kemei has sat through stakeholder meetings, where the agenda of discussion was the impacts of EJEs and EDs in advancing radicalization and violent extremism. The meeting was held on 13th September 2023
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: HURIA participated in a meeting on 13 September with the county stakeholders forum on preventing and countering violent extremism; where HURIA's Executive Director presented on the impact of EJE and EDs in Kwale county in providing fertile ground for violent extremism. The impact included an analysis of EJE and ED cases as documented through the SCC program. The ATPU officer, while present, did not react to this analysis. He spoke on issues of money laundering due to terrorism activities. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, the aforementioned county stakeholder meeting was financially supported through a program funded by the Global centre on cooperative security.
