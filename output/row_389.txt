**Outcome description**: In August 2023, representatives from various political parties in Ninewa responded positively to the demands of families affected by enforced disappearances, including the excavation of graves of ISIS victims. This response took place during the "Right to Know the Truth" conference, organized by the families of the disappeared and the Human Line Foundation.
**Significance of the outcome**: The fate of enforced disappearances was flagged as a priority during community consultations with victims and their families. It is included in the advocacy strategy of the project.For the first time since the liberation of Ninewa, parliamentarians from the region attended a conference to listen to the families of those who were forcibly disappeared and responded supportively to their concerns. They also discussed bringing these issues to the attention of national authorities. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Non-state authorities
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: The Human Line Foundation is a CSO in Ninewa, it was one of the first grantees to be selected by Al-Mesalla. Their staff participated in all trainings and coaching by Al-Mesalla on transitional justice and gender-sensitivity. Two of their staff also actively participated in developing the advocacy strategy, with one of their staff eventually becoming part of the network of advocates, bridging the project's efforts in both objectives. The Human Line Foundation organized a conference on August 22, 2023, which was attended by seven members of the Iraqi Council of Representatives: Shirwan Al-Dobardani, Khalid Sultan Hashem, Rahima Hassan Al-Jubouri, Luqman Al-Rashidi, Jamil Abd Isbak, along with former representatives Mona Al-Obaidi and Hoda Jarallah. The event also saw the participation of Amin Al-Fanash, the former mayor of Mosul, representatives from various government departments in Mosul, and seventy relatives of the forcibly disappeared, including forty women and thirty men. Together, they gathered to voice their concerns and seek information about the whereabouts of their missing family members. The attending parliamentarians committed to advocating for these families both in the Parliament and within the Council of Ministers.
