**Outcome status**: Outcome
**OH-harvesting period**: harvest 2
**coutnry**: DR Congo
**Year**: 2023
**Outcome description**: Following the 2021 training in physical and digital security management add the follow on mentorship and coaching sessions in 2022, in March 2023 Actions pour la Promotion Rurale (APRU) in Haut Uélé province (DRC) integrated preventive security management practices (secure commnunication, risk assessment and risk management) in implementating the exercise of Disarming, Demobilizing, Repatriating, Reintegrating and Resettling (DDRRR) of two groups of the Lord's Resistance Army (LRA) from Zemio and Mboki (CAR) to Northern Uganda sub-region.
**Significance of the outcome**: Before the training in 2021 APRU was not putting in place security management measures in the implementation of its peace building projects. Integrating security mechanisms in (Women) Human Rights Defenders (WHRDs) work helps them to conitnue working safely and securely by minimising the probability of risks to take place and reduce their  impacts on (W)HRDs, their clients and organisations.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: DDRRR is a joint exercise of APRU, PAX and Government of Uganda (Office of the Prime Minister and Ministry of Defence and Veterans Affairs). On March 7th 2023, the Director of APRU contacted DefendDefenders Senior protection Officer and Training Lead to seek guidance on how to securely communicate information about the former LRA soldiers and family members to be repatriated to Uganda from CAR. On 27th March a meeting was held between DefendDefenders (Executive Director) and APRU to identify risks and map out actors in Uganda to mitigate the identified risks. Additionally on 26th October, APRU made a request to DefendDefenders for recommendations of trusted lawyers to support APRU and PAX in their engagements with Ministry of Defence, OPM, Chieftancy of Military Intelligence(CMI) and Uganda People's Defence Forces (UPDF) 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: Yes, its direct, positive and intended. With funding support from the SCC programme, DefendDefenders trained APRU in physical and digital security management in Kampala, Uganda from 8 - 13 November 2021. Additionally DefendDefenders provided continous mentorship and coaching as part of the follow-up process.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: No
