**Outcome description**: Between January - June 2025,  survivors and victims from Ninewa took the leadership in engaging with the media, planning storiy-telling events, and actively planning advocacy events and meetings.
**Significance of the outcome**: Survivors are often excluded from national dialogues, particularly in justice and peacebuilding processes. Our project focused on positioning Iraqi partners and local initiatives to support a victim-centered approach by creating platforms for victims—especially female survivors—to publicly share their stories. This shift not only promotes visibility and recognition but also fosters sustainable engagement between survivors and relevant authorities.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The recods of victims stories  will be used in awareness campaigns and advocacy sessions to strengthen survivor-centered justice approaches in Ninewa.
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: With support from Al-Mesalla, our grantee Kanya supported victims network  and female survivors from Sinjar in sharing their stories through a video series, which was strategically disseminated to maximize visibility and advocacy impact. Both the Iraqi partner and Kanya ensured ethical communication  and survivor-centered engagement throughout the storytelling process and advocacy activities.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Victims and female surviors showed interst to participated and enagae in the TJ process, which led them to participate and learn about TJ and their role as victims in that process.
