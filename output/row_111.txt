**coutnry**: South Sudan
**Year**: 2022
**Outcome description**: After a training on leadership on May 17, 2022, the female members of the Transitional State Legislative Assembly of Upper Nile participated in a meeting which calls for transparency and accountability among members of the local government.
**Significance of the outcome**: The assembly in Malakal is recently formed. Members are brought in via their party. The training was the first training to the assembly. Most women are not educated, are unable to speak English or Arabic language and in the past the women did not actively participate. CDOM-JPC empowered them, during the training the women raised an issue that had to be taken up. Members of the parliament came together and when they discussed the issue the trained women participated and dared to speak up. The deputy of the steering committee dealing with the accountability issue is a women
**Additional information (use if needed) **: NGOs contributed to the training after being invited by CDOM-JPC, ensuring collaboration in peace-work among NGO’s. Presence of UNMISS – UNMISS encouraged CDOM-JPC to conduct these initiatives. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: The training was conducted to women who had joined the parliament for just 3- 4 months. During the training the women were trained on leadership skills. During the training the participants had to conduct groupwork. During the training all women from the parliament came together, before the training the women did not know each other before/did not know how many women are in the parliament. Reference was made during the training to the role of women in the bible, contributing to the spirit of service. Women were encouraged to speak up for the needs of her community.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: no
