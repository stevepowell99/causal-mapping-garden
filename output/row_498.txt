**Outcome description**: In May 2024, members of the Network of Advocates (Azad) launched a victim-led organization called Anin Organization, specializing in the field of transitional justice and inclusive peacebuilding in Ninewa. 
**Significance of the outcome**: This marks a significant and sustainable step toward continuing support for victims and survivors from Ninewa. There was a significant gap in knowledge on transformative justice and development among excluded youth and in the whole Civil Society. In 2022, many of these CSOs and activists were primarily focused on humanitarian work, often lacking gender balance, inclusive approaches, and ethical engagement. They saw this initiative as an opportunity to shift their focus toward reparations and rights-based work
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Azad, a survivor from a rural area in the Ninewa Plain, registered a volunteer team in Nimrud. Reham, a young Yezidi woman, now works as a facilitator for sessions focused on Yezidi survivors. Lahun, a group of young women who initially volunteered, is currently implementing in their NGO around five projects related to education, legal rights, and reparations. Nasr, a Yezidi man, began providing trainings on storytelling in 2025, contributing to healing and awareness efforts, Salam started to train on digital transformation for the government, Raniya started her work on Education rights in rural areas with INGO..
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: Over the past four years, a network of activists and volunteer teams has been established, with members participating in around 150 activities. They have undergone a comprehensive capacity-building process that included trainings, coaching, implementing initiatives, building wide relationships, networking and engaging in reflective practices.
