**Outcome description**: In the first part of 2025, Ahimsa Centre extended its outreach to train non-violent activists in the city of Raqqa and Deirezzor as a means to building an inclusive network of non-violent activists in Eastern Syria.
**Significance of the outcome**: Ahimsa Centre was formed in Hassake in 2023 and focused on training and working with non-violent activists in this city. As part of clarifying its work strategy, it identified the need to diversify its network of activists and to include activists originating from other regions in NES to provide opportunities for a more diverse dialogue. In 2025, Ahimsa Centre reached out to youth groups operating in Raqqa and Deirezzor seeking cooperation in order to provide opportunity for young women and men originating from these 2 cities to join Ahimsa Centre's activities. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Organisation
**Contribution of the programme**: Through the SCC project, PAX provided mentoring and coaching to Ahimsa Centre towards institutional development and strategy development. Ahimsa Centre recognized the need to expand its networks and seek to work with young women and men from other areas in NES. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: D&P funds a project of PAX called Kulluna Muwatinun. It is through this project that Ahimsa has been able to propose organizing non-violent action trainings to young women and men living in Raqqa and Deirezzor.
