**Outcome description**: In May 2025, at the UNSC Protection of Civilians (PoC) week in New York,  new PoC report by UN Secretary General sustained strong references to environmental damage from conflicts as a source of civilian harm, while a substantial number of States recognized the need to prevent and tackle environmental harm in conflicts in their statements during the PoC debate and related Arria Formula meeting. 
**Significance of the outcome**: As in the previous year, this development helps to anchor the incorporation of the EPS agenda into the PoC agenda and debates, placing this issue increasingly within the purview of UNSC Council - despite the opposition from certain Council Members and contributing to the consolidation of international attention and support for addressing it. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: International community
**Scale**: International 
**Contribution of the programme**: PAX's contribution is direct and indirect. On the one hand, PAX provided input to the Secretary -General's team working on the report, ensuring the key environmental issues are reflected, and shared related policy brief among Member Sattes. On the other hand, PAX's long-standing work on the issue has been helping to raise awareness about it for years among Member States, which indirectly paves the ground for more political recognition.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, there are numerous other actors involved in pushing for the recognition of the EPS-related issues on the UN level, including champions among Member States, IOs, and other civil society actors.
**Rating contribution**: little
**The change was unintended**: NO
