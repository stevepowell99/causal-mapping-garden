**Outcome description**: On 22 May 2024, Ahmed Elgarrai Sojoud Omer, a woman human rights defender (WHRD) from Sudan was elected as the Chairperson of the Exiled HRD working group in Uganda.
**Significance of the outcome**: The election of Sojoud reflects the integration of a gender perspective into the leadership structures of the working group (WG). The role also enables the WHRD to build her capacity not only in leading the working group but to also effectively advocate for protection of (W)HRDs, including gender-specific needs.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Sojoud is a Sudanese WHRD working with African Center for Rights and Development. The organisation documented the violations that women faced in Sudan such as child marriage and Female Genital Mutilation. They collaborate with Karama organization and Al Khatim Adlan Center for Enlightenment and Human Rights (KACE). On 20th April 2023 during the Sudan war she was targeted by security authorities because of a photograph she took depicting the violence that women were subjected to. Her camera was taken and she was threatened. She was able to relocate to Uganda where she submitted a request for protection to DefendDefenders .
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: With funding support from the programme, DefendDefenders provided  living costs to support the WHRD. With this she was able to cater for her basic needs and rent a safe place while in Uganda. Additionally, Sojoud participated in the regular WG meetings and took part in trainings (advocacy, security management) enabling her to continue engaging in human rights work more effectively. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes, the (W)HRD received support from DefendDefenders' other projects i.e, the UNWomen Peace and Humanitarian Fund (WPHF) and the Dutch funded Sudan project. 
**Rating contribution**: moderate
**The change was unintended**: YES
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: On request, report/minutes of the Exiled HRD working group meeting can be shared. We can also provide the contact  for the WHRD and an interview can be arranged.
