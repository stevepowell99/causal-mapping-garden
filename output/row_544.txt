**Outcome description**: Between 2023 and 2025, three separate groups of men in Ninewa initiated grassroots collaboration targeting other male community members and leaders, addressing gender inequality, advocating for women's education rights, and combatting violence against women. 
**Significance of the outcome**: PFO documneted the following instances: 2023: In old Mosul city: a WhatsApp group established by Men community leaders, to combat domestic violence against women with Sawt team. 2024, In Nimrud: Aneen Volunteer team (mostly men) work on education right for girls, then in 2025 Mosul. 2024 Tal kef "Hope Colors" team worked on education rights and 3 women joint their volunteer team. These initiatives reflect a shift in community norms, where male-led groups are increasingly engaging in gender equality and advocating for women's rights. In transformative justice theory, this signifies a critical change: powerholders and community members who were previously passive or complicit are now taking active roles in addressing structural harms and supporting collective healing, by mobilizing men in grassroots efforts

**Contribution of the programme**: since 2022, the project supported these groups through series of trainings, dialogue spaces, and community engagement tools that encouraged men’s active role in gender justice and advocacy. In Mosul city, a social initiatives conducted, (sessions and booklet) for the community leaders about violence against women.
