**Outcome description**: In September 2022, residents of Bashiqa bought olive oil and bread from residents of Al-Fadiliya, and they made official agreements to initiate commercial trade between producers in Al Fadiliya village and the markets of Bashiqa and Mosul
**Significance of the outcome**: Prior to this initiative, commercial trade between these two areas had completely halted since the invasion of ISIS in 2014, and had not resumed due to sectarian tensions and mistrust between the residents of both areas. Following this agreement, partner staff residing in Bashiqa reported an increased occurence of visits from both sides and more tolerance towards residents of the other village. Additionally, the trade agreement resulted in the creation of job opprotunities for 3 women in Al Fadiliya
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: One of the grantees within the project was given a grant to organize a bazar in September 2022 to display locally produced agricultural and dairy products. The exhibition was held in the Peace center of Bashiqa and many producers from Al Fadiliya were invited to display their products in Bashiqa for the first time in several years. The grantee had participated in the workshop on conflict and gender analysis organized by partner PFO and decided to tackle the tensions between these two villages through economic relationships. The grantee also ensured female producers and market owners were given a large space to participate in the activitiy
