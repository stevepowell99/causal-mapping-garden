**Outcome description**: In 2025, a family from Bethlehem transfered their family’s legacy for safekeeping and documentation in Jerusalem which was experienced as a form of victory a reclaiming of narrative, space, and historical presence.
**Significance of the outcome**: The previous group of trainees originating from Jerusalem, the Golan Heights, Nazareth, Ramla, Baqa al-Gharbiyye, and other Palestinian localities, travelled to Bethlehem in the West Bank to collect the mentioned family archive. The act of crossing the check points and collaborating on the preservation of a rich family archive including documentation of ancestral properties in Jaffa was deeply meaningful for both the trainees and the family. It demonstrated how, despite occupation, checkpoints, and systemic fragmentation, collective memory work can overcome barriers. Countering efforts to reduce civic space is at the core of the SO. reclaiming space is therefore significant. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: The visit by the trainees and collecting the material was part of the project.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes. The contribution of Palestinian families who entrusted their archives played a pivotal role in enabling this change. Additionally, the broader political context including movement restrictions imposed by the occupation; indirectly strengthened the group’s sense of purpose and solidarity, highlighting the importance of cross-regional collaboration in archival work.
