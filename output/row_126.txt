**Outcome description**: Following advocacy  of CBDDH, ROADDH, DefendDefenders, Ubuntu Hub City Côte d'Ivoire and the Alapini Foundation (ICC magistrate) with the government of Burkina Faso in December 2022 and early January 2023, the Minister of Communication and Government Spokesperson has pledged that the government of Burkina Faso is now ready to receive complaints from HRDs at risk.
**Significance of the outcome**: The protection of human rights defenders is important to guarantee their safety and the continuation of their activities. For a long time, the government of Burkina Faso has ignored calls from HRDs to receive cases of HRDs at risk and to ensure that they are protected. This prompted the CBDDH, DefendDefenders and the hub town Ubuntubased in Côte d'Ivoire to engage in advocacy and put pressure on the government to act. The government officially declared its readiness to protect human rights defenders in a statement by the Minister of Communication and Government Spokesperson..
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Since this statement, the CBDDH has received information from reliable sources that the organisation and its staff are in the crosshairs of the intelligence and security services. These threats are in addition to those made by the former Director General of Human Rights on 18 June 2021. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: The programme activated regional and international mechanisms for the protection of HRDs in order to maximise the chances of changing the government's position in favour of the protection of HRDs. The programme also called on the government of Burkina Faso to take action to protect human rights defenders.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Réseau des défenseurs des droits de l'homme en Afrique de l'Ouest (WAHRDN/ROADDH)
