**Outcome description**: In December 2022, 40 Human Rights Defenders launched the Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders with Disabilities Network
**Significance of the outcome**: Before the training and launching of Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders with Disabilities Network,  Human Rights Defenders with disaibility silently faced challenges and threats. There was no networking and collaboration among Human Rights Defenders with disability and lack of inclusion of Human Rights Defenders with Defenders. Without the network it was difficult for the members to identify themselves as Human Rights Defenders and work together in solidarity.   
**Additional information (use if needed) **: A training on basic human rights, human rights advocacy, and advocacy on disability rights was conducted for 40 Human Rights Defenders with diabilities  from several regions in the country. On the last day of the training the first Human Rights Defenders with Disabilities Network was launched and 7 members of the steering committe were elected by the General Assembly. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: The training was conducted by EHRDC in partnership with UN OHCHR in Ethiopia in December 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**:  Ethiopia Human Rights Center, Ethiopia Women with Disabilities National Association, Ethiopia Lawyers with Disability Association, and United Nations Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (Ethiopia)
**Rating contribution**: great
