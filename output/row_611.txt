**Outcome description**: in 2025 the Mitvim–SWP partnership strenghtening and enlarged the network of Mitvim by a residency of one of the Mitvim researchers in Berlin as well as plans for a joint policy dialogue involving more colleagues later in 2025.
**Significance of the outcome**: The flourishing partnership between Mitvim and the German think tank SWP led among other things to the convening of a high-level expert discussion on the impact of the U.S.-China rivalry on Middle Eastern dynamics. The roundtable featured 15 leading experts from Iran, Germany, Saudi Arabia, and Israel. Key contributions included Dr. Hamidreza Azizi on Iran’s nuclear file, Dr. Aziz Alghashian on Saudi strategic diversification, and Brian Katulis on the U.S. regional role. This collaboration resulted in a robust body of analysis connecting global geopolitical shifts to regional realities.

**Contribution of the programme**: The SCC project facilitated deepened ties between Mitvim and SWP, allowing for sustained collaboration through joint policy planning and regional convenings. This partnership led directly to the co-organization of a high-level roundtable in May 2025 that brought together 15 experts from across the MENA region and Europe to address U.S.-China tensions and their implications for the Middle East. Mitvim coordinated Israeli participation, co-shaped the agenda, and contributed regional framing.
