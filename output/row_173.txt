**Outcome description**: In 2022,  the "Ethiopia Intelligence and security apparatus" released  6 Human Rights Defenders including Journalists. They regained their freedom following advocacy campaigns led by Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders Center. The journalists had been kidnapped and illegally detained in Ethiopia. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Before the campaigns, 6 Human Rights Defenders including journalists had been arrested and illegally detained in undisclosed places. They also had no access to their lawyers or families. Without the campaigns and pressure they would have remained incarcerated. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders Center also developed and published press statements seeking the release of the Human Rights Defenders including Journalists. Multiple online campaigns were launched to exert pressure on government to disclose detention centers, allow access to family members and lawyers and their unconditional release. 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: Individual
**Contribution of the programme**: The EHRDC developed and published press statements seeking the release of HRDs including journalists. EHRDC provided a lawyer
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Other Civil Society Organisations, media and Ethiopian Human Rights Commission. Other Civil Society Organisations and the Ethiopia Human Rights Commission also released statements on behalf of the detained and kidnapped Human Rights Defenders including Journalists. Media also circulated campaings led by Ethiopia Human Rights Defenders Center.  
**Rating contribution**: moderate
**The change was unintended**: NO
**Evidence supporting the outcome statement**: The campaigns were circulated on media platforms such TIKVAH, Addis Standard newsletters and other medias and influencial pages.
