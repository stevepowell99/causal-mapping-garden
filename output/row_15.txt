**Outcome description**: In October 2021, the Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network was launched to create synergy among women human rights defenders to provide Women Human Rights Defenders related capacity building trainings, create a platform to share experiences, resources and information in Ethiopia. There were 30 participants from various CSOs and individual Women human rights defenders, advocating for women's rights from across Ethiopia, including Oromia, Amhara, Addis Ababa, SNNP, and Benishangul. Three males and 29 female participants took part in the training.  
**Significance of the outcome**: Before the establishment of the Network, there was lack of coordination and a safe platform for Women Human Rights Defenders to discuss and share issues pertinent to them. The Network is significant in mobilizing resources and providing tailor made solutions and opportunities to Women Human Rights Defenders. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The network was launched during the capacity building event in October, 2021 in Addis Ababa. It was established during the East African Women Human Rights Defenders Network - General Assembly. In addition to this, by-laws, rules and regulations were developed for operationalization of the Ethiopian Women Human Rights Defenders Network. 
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: The program facilitated the East African Women Human Rrights Defenders Network - General Assembly that established the Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network. The program also funded the launching of Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network. The EHRDC and UN OHCHR in Ethiopia facilitated this process
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: United Nations Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights ( Ethiopia) contributed financially to the launch of Ethiopia Women Human Rights Defenders Network. The Ethiopia Human Rights Center, individual women human rights defenders, women led Civil Society Organisations and Civil Society Organisations working on women Rights.
