**Outcome description**: In 2022, two grantees from the Arab Sunni community in Mosul provided a platform for Yazidi female survivors to share stories of their suffering with an audience in old Mosul (mostly Muslim Sunni).
**Significance of the outcome**: Female survivors of ISIS captivity and sexual violence from the Yazidi and other minority communities suffered great harm under ISIS. And after Mosul city liberation, and for the first time, two grantees (Our Hope and Humanitarian Line) from Mosul and Muslim Sunni backgrounds organized storytelling session where Yazidi female survivors of genocide could share their stories with Sunni community from old Mosul city. Its important for the Mosul as Muslim Sunni community to meet with these Yazidi female survivors from Sinjar and listen their stories and express their empathy and support that help the survivors in their first step of healing journey, also its safe space for Yazidi female survivors to raise their voice as first step for the advocacy process to reach their reparations rights and justice.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: On the 8th anniversary of the Yazidi genocide, on August 3, 2022, our grantees (Our Hope, Jood, and The Humanitarian Line) held a joint solidarity session with the participation of three Yazidis to share their stories, for the first time since Ninewa liberation from ISIS to various local media channels, activists, local and international organization, and relevant local government in Mosul and the High Commission for Human Rights office in Mosul. This initiative resulted solidarity with the Yazidi community by the participants and reflect to build bridges of brotherhood and peace and not to repeat what happened with new generation as part of transformative justice in Ninewa.
