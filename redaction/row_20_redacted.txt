**Outcome description**: In <redacted>, parliamentarians from the Budget and Economic Committee recommended the formation of a joint Working Group of parliamentarians and civil society actors to examine and contribute to the public finance management process. This was done in the parliment during a meeting of the Budget Committee. 
**Significance of the outcome**: The recommendation for the formation of this working group demonstrates a willingness for a more inclusive public finance review process. This has been the first time that such recommendation was done in the Budget and Economic Committee. There are no other commission within the parliament but only the Budget and Economic Committee who is working on this.

**Contribution of the programme**: SSUDEMOP held 5 strategic meetings <redacted> with parliamentarians and civil society actors to examine key components of public finance. During these meetings SSUDEMOP presented an assessment report on the taxation system in South Sudan, a policy brief on harmonization of the South Sudan tax system and a comparative analysis of financial bills from the <redacted> region. These engagements enhanced relationship between civil society actors and parliamentarians. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: SSUDEMOP isn’t aware of any other Civil Organization that is working on the Budget and Economic Bill. The only parliamentary group working on the Budget and Economic Commission are the committee and no other.
