**Outcome description**: In 2025 HURIA led the first CSO consultative process of drafting a legislation on the criminal prohibition of Enforced Dissaperances (ED) in Kenya. The draft bill not only seeks to define the crime and its penalties but most importantly, it has incoporated provisions that address victims needs such as reparations.
**Significance of the outcome**: HURIA has managed to expand and deepen consultations among CSO actors on the need for Criminalisiation of ED through a legislative proposal and spearheaded the drafting of the Legislation. As this is the first convening of CSOs in Kenya to deliberate on <person> and the formulation of a legislative proposal, this process strengthens the rule of law, enhances protection for victims and their families, and aligns Kenya’s domestic legal framework with international human rights obligations, such as the International Convention for the Protection of All Persons from Enforced Disappearance.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Spearheading the drafting of the legislation positions HURIA as a key driver in shaping a legal framework that can provide clear definitions, establish accountability <redacted>, and create enforceable penalties against ED. Since 2024, HURIA has been organising a series of consultative meetings in <LOC> with a range of actors, including national institutions, to analyze the contents of the proposed bill and deliberate on key emerging issues. These meetings concluded with critical inputs and recommendations, particularly emphasizing the need to criminalize enforced disappearance in Kenya and have resulted to the development of a draft bill.
