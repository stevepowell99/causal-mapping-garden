**Outcome description**: In 2023 the Juba Massacre Widows Association, with support from the strengthening civil courage project offered psychosocial and medical (Mental health) support for the widows and orphans with special needs which helped improve relations between them and their families leading to healing and reconciliation within the families. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Due to the high trauma level between the widows and orphans of concidering wht they experienced following the disappearance of their fathers and husbands during the 1992 Juba Massacre, some of the orphans and widows resorted  alcoholism and became very violent whichgreatly  strained their relations and led to conflict within the families . 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Civic change agents
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: The financial and technical support from partners such as PAX for peace, through the Power of Voices project the Juba Massacre Widows Association was able to help by providing psychosocial support and medical care to some of the widows and orphans of the victims of the 1992 Juba Massacre. This helped them address any underlying health issues and address some of the psychological traumas they experienced following the loss of their fathers and husbands.
