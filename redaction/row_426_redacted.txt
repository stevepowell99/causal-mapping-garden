**Outcome description**: In <redacted>, the EU Member States and the European Parliament reached a provisional agreement on a final text for the Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive. It explicitly excludes the financial sector and the arms sector from having to conduct HRDD. This is a negative outcome.
**Significance of the outcome**: The fact that the financial sector as well as the arms sector will be explicitly excluded from the CSDDD is a missed opportunity and risks sending the wrong signal to these actors (namely, that they are less scrutinized when it comes to their human rights performance). 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: International community
**Scale**: International
**Contribution of the programme**: PAX contributed to advocacy efforts by civil society to have the financial sector and the arms sector included in the scope of the legislation. However, these advocacy efforts did not lead to positive results.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: The exclusion of the financial sector and the arms sector is mainly due to strong corporate lobby and the influence of some member states (e.g. France)
