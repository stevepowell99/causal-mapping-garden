**Outcome description**: Since <redacted>, community members in <LOC> have been discussing human rights violations with local security agents and oversight mechanisms in order to minimize and seek solutions to address gross human rights violations such as disapearances, torture, extra judicial executions. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Before <redacted>, there was no platform for community members to express their frustrations on continuous human rights violations and discuss ways to address the problem with the respective authorities. This led to the idea to establish Chief <person>  where communities can raise their security concerns and request local chiefs to be more proactive in addressing these issues. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: n/a 
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: Engaged citizens
**Scale**: Local
**Contribution of the programme**: With support of SCC Kenya programme, in <redacted> and <redacted>, Haki na Sheria (HSI) has organised two community-security dialogues in which community members interacted with various state agents allegedely perpetrating gross human right violations, in particular the representatives from the Anti-terror police unit (<person>). Furthermore, the dialogues were attended by government offcials in charge of responding to these incidents, in particular the office of the <LOC> Commissioner (more especifcally assistant County Commissioner), Department of Criminal Investigation (DCI), Independent Police <person> (IPOA) and Kenya Prisons in <LOC>. Communities members and local HRDs network were able to outrightly express their opinions on the current violations by state agents who were represented at the dialogue.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Actors such as Human Rights Defenders Network - Garissa Chapter, Garisa Peace Committee, National Police service, <LOC> Commissioner, IPOA, DCI participated in the discussions and were positive about this initiative, suggesting even to expand the initiative to other sub-counties in <LOC>.
