**Outcome description**: In Burkina Faso, <redacted>, approximately 350 terrorism cases were tried by the Ouaga II High Court, with more than 200 convictions
**Significance of the outcome**: From 2021 to 2023, only 68 terrorism cases were tried despite an increase in their number in the courts. This <redacted> a sense of impunity and slowness of justice among victims and public opinion. The fact that around 350 terrorism cases were tried between <redacted> 2024 marks a significant change in the judicial response to terrorism and the fight against impunity for terrorist acts.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Several of the judgments handed down concern cases of abuses committed by terrorists against civilian populations in the northern, north-central and <LOC> regions.
**Rating significance**: large
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: Monitoring activities carried out by CIFDHA with the support of the AINL have made it possible to document human rights violations perpetrated by terrorist groups. Based on these findings, one of the main focuses of CIFDHA's advocacy with the authorities is to speed up the judicial processing of terrorism cases in order to guarantee better access to justice for victims. CIFDHA has reiterated this recommendation on several occasions in its interactions with the Ministry of Justice, in the alternative reports it has submitted to the Human Rights Council under the fourth cycle of the Universal Periodic Review (UPR), to the Committee against Torture and to the Human Rights Committee. In their concluding observations, the Human Rights Council and these treaty bodies made the same recommendation to the State of Burkina Faso.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: Yes. Other human rights actors such as civil society organisations and PTFs have also advocated for the acceleration of the trial of terrorism cases.
