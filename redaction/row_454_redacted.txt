**Outcome description**: On <redacted>, the National Minister of the Interior sent a telegram (No. 124) to all governors asking them to report on cases of illegal roadblocks and those maintaining them following a complaint by the Secretary General of ASSODIP.
**Significance of the outcome**: Prior to this complaint, the national dimension of the problem of illegal roadblocks had not yet been officially recognised by the national authorities and had never been given such attention. Roadblocks are often illegal, serving to extort and harrass passersby, and are frequently associated with human rights abuses.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: This reaction from the Minister of the Interior came after a complaint was made on <redacted> by the Secretary General of ASSODIP to <redacted> officials representing the Government at the pre-session meetings of the Universal Periodic Review in <LOC> about violations of freedom of movement and arbitrary arrests at illegal roadblocks. On that occasion, the President of the National Human Rights Commission promised to refer the matter urgently to the competent government authority. It should be noted that the complaint was also made to the diplomatic missions accredited to the United Nations present at the meeting.
