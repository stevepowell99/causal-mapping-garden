**Outcome description**: Since <redacted>, the National Commission of Human Rights in Burkina Faso rejected collaborative initiatives with the Burkina Faso National Coalition of HRDs following the submission of their report with alternative views on the situation of HRDs to the UN HRC. The Coalition's report contradicted the findings of the report that was submitted by the NCHR to the UPR process of the UNHRC, regarding the state of HR and HRDs in the country.
**Significance of the outcome**: Prior to 2023, collaborative relations between the human rights institution and the national coalition of HRDs were good. They organized joint <redacted> on human rights issues. The institution supported the coalition in the implementation of its <redacted> but also involved and/or consulted the national coalition on specific aspects of human rights. In light of the 4th cycle of the UPR process (<redacted>), CBDDH submitted an alternative report in <redacted> to the UNHRC with key observations on the conduct of the commission and recommendations. These findings were not discussed with the Commission on forehand, so the Commission felt humiliated as the report contradicted what they had submitted in their report for the UPR process.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: After the submission of the report with key observations on the conduct of the commission, further collaborative efforts have been ignored. The national coalition, in its collaborative approach, has sent requests for an audience with the national human rights institution but these have not been answered. The national institution also refused to organize a joint activity with the national coalition.
