**Outcome description**: After the Training on <redacted> for Women Human Rights defenders in <redacted>, the members of Ethiopian Women Human Rights Defenders Network in Ethiopia implemented safety and security measures to reduce their risks and vulnerabilities caused by their human rights work. 
**Significance of the outcome**: Members of EWHRDN in Ethiopia  have come under threats and risks as a result of glaring gaps in capacity to engage with different local, regional and international  protection mechanisms for WHRDs.  Gaps have equally existed in physical and digital security to aid them navigate the turbulent environment  around human rights work. The training on protection mechanisms was very important to the WHRDs by enabling them to identify risks, minimize risks and threats as well as enable them to continue engaging in humanr giths work safely. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The EWHRDN members mentioned some of the risks associated with their work in Ethiopia to include: Arrests, Criminalization, Verbal Harassments, Physical assault, Rape, Forced migration, Online harassments, Discrimination, Losing jobs, Exclusion,  Murder, Torture, Kidnapping, Losing marriage/ family and Labeling. Setaset Power, a member of EWHRDN and an afro feminist movement operates online media platforms with potential online threats and risks. This group  has been facing alot of backlash, online and offline harassments and labeling. Equally, other members of the EWHRDN who work in conflict areas are faced with a lot of security challenges and did not know how to navigate them. The training was very informative and EWHRDN members who participated reported an better understanding and knowledge on security planning, assessment and management and gave them more confidence to engage in human rights work.
