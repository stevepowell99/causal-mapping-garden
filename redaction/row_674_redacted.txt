**Outcome description**: Following a panel <redacted>, organized by CARD in <redacted> in <LOC> on their first-ever consolidated human rights report addressing the violations occurring in <LOC>, 15 prominent online and mainstream media outlets gave the report significant coverage, and CARD was invited to present its findings at one  local and one international forum.
**Significance of the outcome**: Previously, human rights violations in <LOC> went unreported, and communities remained silent out of fear or lack of platforms to share their concerns. The panel brought together 53 participants, including community representatives, local human rights organizations, the EHRC, the Ministry of Justice, and media outlets. It provided a platform to openly address the violations occurring in <LOC>; brought victims’ stories to light; and prompted commitments to pursue justice, advancing the project’s goal of raising awareness and securing recognition for affected communities. 
**Additional information (use if needed) **: Locally, the event was featured in a zoom conference organized by TIMRAN, which engaged community stakeholders and civil society representatives. Internationally, the <redacted> Peace building Network (EPBN) co-chaired by IOM and Norwegian Refugee Council CARD highlighted the event through its platform, sharing key outcomes and insights of the report with the broader network.
**Rating significance**: early/small
**Social actor**: Media
**Scale**: National 
**Contribution of the programme**: With the support of AINL, under the SCC programme, CARD was able to recruit journalists and train them on monitoring, documentation and reporting of human rights violations and abuses.  The journalists then compiled the evidence of human rights violations/abuses in <LOC>, which CARD used to produce its human rights report. Once finalized, CARD organized and hosted the discussion on the report. 
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: No
**Rating contribution**: great
