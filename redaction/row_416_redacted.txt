**Outcome description**: In Mali in 2023, following the 187 recommendations made during the 2018 Universal Periodic Review, the Ministry of Justice and Human Rights, Keeper of the Seals, involved several human rights organisations, including TRIJEUD-Mali and COMADDH, in the preparatory workshops (held on 13 and <redacted> respectively at <LOC> in <LOC>, and on 5 and <redacted> at <LOC>) on the drafting of the national report for the 4th cycle of the Universal Periodic Review, and took account of the recommendations made by the ODDH in the draft bill on the Criminal Code and the Code of Criminal Procedure on <redacted>, incorporating provisions such as the criminalisation of slavery, human trafficking and the smuggling of migrants.
**Significance of the outcome**: Prior to the collaboration with human rights organisations, the process of drafting the national report for the Universal Periodic Review was mainly internal to the Ministry of Justice and Human Rights, with limited participation from external parties. The involvement of human rights organisations in the preparatory workshops for the national UPR report represents a significant change as it strengthens the legitimacy and credibility of the human rights assessment process.
**Additional information (use if needed) **: The collaboration between the government and the SCC programme partners has created a positive dynamic for the implementation of the report's recommendations, by fostering collective ownership of the objectives and actions to be taken to improve the human rights situation in the country.
**Rating significance**: medium
**Social actor**: State authorities
**Scale**: National
**Contribution of the programme**: Following the 187 recommendations made by <LOC> during the 2018 Universal Periodic Review, the TRIJEUD, taking into account the data collected as part of the MDR project, has focused its contribution on five key points, in particular the prosecution of perpetrators of serious human rights violations, women's rights, children's rights, the practice of slavery and the death penalty.
**Contribution of other actors and factors**: In addition to the SCC programme partners, other CSOs such as WILDAF and TEMEDT were also able to make recommendations.
